## Why Start with This Question?

Before we explore how AI can help in teaching, we need to establish a clear understanding of what AI actually is — and what it is not. Misconceptions about AI are everywhere, and having a solid foundation will make everything else in this course easier to understand.

## What AI Is — and What It Is Not

Artificial intelligence is a field of computer science focused on developing systems that can perform tasks typically associated with human cognitive abilities: recognizing patterns, understanding language, making decisions, and generating content.

However, the term "intelligence" can be misleading. AI systems don't think, feel, or understand the way humans do. They process information through mathematical operations on large amounts of data.

> **Key Insight**: AI does not think like humans. It processes information through mathematical operations on large amounts of data.

### What AI Can Do

- Recognize patterns in text, images, and data
- Generate human-like text, images, and audio
- Translate between languages
- Summarize long documents
- Answer questions based on learned patterns

### What AI Cannot Do

- Truly understand meaning or context the way humans do
- Feel emotions or have genuine opinions
- Guarantee accuracy — AI can and does make mistakes
- Replace human judgment, creativity, and empathy

## Common Myths About AI

**Myth 1: "AI knows everything."**
AI models are trained on data up to a certain date. They don't have access to real-time information unless specifically connected to the internet, and they can generate incorrect information with complete confidence.

**Myth 2: "AI is going to take our jobs."**
AI is a tool. Like all tools, it changes how we work rather than simply replacing us. Teachers who understand AI will be better equipped to prepare students for a world where AI is commonplace.

**Myth 3: "AI understands what I'm saying."**
AI processes patterns in language. It can produce remarkably relevant responses, but this is statistical pattern matching, not comprehension.

## What About Chatbots?

A chatbot is a *user interface* — a way for humans to interact with an AI system through conversation. The chatbot itself is not "the AI." Behind it are large language models (LLMs), which are the actual AI systems that process and generate text.

Think of it this way: a chatbot is like a phone — it's the device you use to communicate. The LLM is the person (or in this case, the system) on the other end.

## Narrow AI vs. General AI

The AI we use today is **narrow AI** (also called weak AI) — it's designed for specific tasks. ChatGPT is very good at generating text, but it can't drive a car. An image recognition system can identify objects in photos, but it can't write poetry.

**Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)** — AI that can do anything a human can do — does not yet exist. All current AI tools are narrow AI, no matter how impressive they seem.

## Which Parts of AI Are Already in Education?

You may already be encountering AI in education without realizing it:

| Application | Example | AI Type |
|---|---|---|
| Adaptive learning | Platforms that adjust difficulty based on student performance | Machine learning |
| Automated grading | Systems that grade multiple-choice tests | Pattern recognition |
| Language translation | Google Translate, DeepL | Neural machine translation |
| Writing assistance | Grammar checkers like Grammarly | Natural language processing |
| Content generation | ChatGPT, Claude | Large language models |

## Why This Matters for Teachers

Teachers already possess many competencies that transfer directly to working with AI:

- **Defining clear goals** — just like writing a lesson plan, working with AI requires clarity about what you want
- **Structuring tasks** — breaking down complex assignments into steps is exactly how you get the best results from AI
- **Evaluating outputs** — teachers are experts at assessing quality, which is essential when using AI-generated content
- **Adapting based on results** — iterating and improving is something teachers do every day

Understanding what AI is helps you make informed decisions about when and how to use it in your classroom.

## AI Is Already in Your School

Even if you haven't intentionally started using AI tools, chances are that AI is already part of your school environment. From the spell-checker in your word processor to the recommendation algorithms in educational platforms, AI powers many tools you use daily.

## Adaptive Learning Platforms

One of the most established uses of AI in education is **adaptive learning**. These are platforms that adjust the difficulty and content based on how a student performs:

- If a student gets several math problems right, the system increases the difficulty
- If they struggle, it provides additional practice at the current level
- The system tracks patterns over time to personalize the learning path

Examples include platforms like Khan Academy, Duolingo, and many national educational platforms.

## Automated Assessment

AI-powered assessment tools can:

- Grade multiple-choice and fill-in-the-blank tests instantly
- Provide basic feedback on writing (grammar, spelling, structure)
- Detect patterns in student performance across a class

However, automated grading has significant limitations. It cannot assess creativity, critical thinking, or the nuanced understanding that a human teacher evaluates in essays and projects.

## Content Creation and Curation

Teachers are increasingly using AI to:

- **Generate** lesson plans, worksheets, and quiz questions
- **Adapt** materials for different reading levels
- **Translate** content for multilingual classrooms
- **Summarize** long texts for student use

## Teacher Competencies That Transfer to AI

The good news is that effective teaching skills translate directly to effective AI use:

| Teaching Skill | AI Application |
|---|---|
| Writing clear learning objectives | Writing clear prompts for AI |
| Differentiating instruction | Asking AI to adapt content for different levels |
| Assessing student work | Evaluating AI-generated content for quality |
| Giving specific feedback | Providing specific instructions to improve AI output |

## What's Changing

The introduction of large language models like ChatGPT and Claude has accelerated AI adoption in education. Unlike previous AI tools that served narrow purposes, these models can assist with a wide range of tasks — making them both more useful and more challenging to navigate.

> **For Teachers**: The question is not whether AI will be part of education, but how we can use it thoughtfully and effectively.

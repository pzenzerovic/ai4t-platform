## Getting Started

ChatGPT is one of the most widely used AI tools. This lesson walks you through getting started and using it effectively for teaching tasks.

## Setting Up

1. Visit chat.openai.com
2. Create a free account using your email
3. The free version (GPT-3.5) is sufficient for most teaching tasks
4. GPT-4 (paid) offers improved reasoning and accuracy

## Your First Teaching Prompt

Try this starter prompt to see ChatGPT in action:

*"I am a [subject] teacher working with [grade level] students. I need to create a [type of material] on the topic of [topic]. The material should be appropriate for [time duration] and should include [specific requirements]."*

Fill in the brackets with your actual needs and see what ChatGPT produces.

## Practical Use Cases for Teachers

### Lesson Planning
Ask ChatGPT to draft lesson plans, including learning objectives, activities, and assessment ideas.

### Creating Differentiated Materials
Request the same content at different reading levels or with different types of support.

### Generating Assessment Items
Create quiz questions, discussion prompts, or project rubrics.

### Simplifying Complex Texts
Paste a complex article and ask ChatGPT to rewrite it for your students' level.

## Tips for Better Results

- **Be specific** about grade level and subject
- **Iterate** — refine your prompt based on the first result
- **Ask for alternatives** — "Give me 3 different approaches to..."
- **Set the format** — "Present this as a table/list/paragraph"
- **Review everything** — always check AI output before using it

## Important Reminders

- Never share student personal data with ChatGPT
- Always review generated content for accuracy
- Use ChatGPT as a starting point, not a final product
- Be aware that ChatGPT's knowledge has a cutoff date

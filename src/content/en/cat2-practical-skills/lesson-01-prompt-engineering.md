## What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt engineering is the skill of formulating clear, specific instructions to get better results from AI tools. Think of it as learning to communicate effectively with a very capable but literal assistant.

Just as you would give clear instructions to a substitute teacher, the quality of what you get from AI depends heavily on the quality of your request.

## The Basics: Why Specificity Matters

Compare these two prompts:

**Vague prompt**: "Make a worksheet about photosynthesis"

**Specific prompt**: "Create a worksheet for 6th graders on photosynthesis, including 3 diagram-labeling tasks and 5 multiple-choice questions at basic comprehension level, suitable for a 20-minute activity"

The second prompt will produce dramatically better results because it specifies:
- **Audience**: 6th graders
- **Topic**: photosynthesis
- **Format**: diagrams and multiple-choice
- **Quantity**: 3 + 5 tasks
- **Level**: basic comprehension
- **Constraint**: 20-minute activity

## Five Key Elements of a Good Prompt

### 1. Role

Tell the AI what role to assume:
- "You are an experienced 8th-grade science teacher"
- "Act as a reading specialist for elementary students"

### 2. Task

Be explicit about what you want:
- "Create a lesson plan" (not "help me with a lesson")
- "Write 10 discussion questions" (not "give me some questions")

### 3. Context

Provide relevant background:
- "My students are 12-13 years old, B1 English level"
- "This is for a 45-minute class with no lab equipment"

### 4. Format

Specify the output structure:
- "Present this as a numbered list"
- "Format as a table with columns for..."
- "Keep each point to 2-3 sentences"

### 5. Constraints

Set boundaries:
- "Use vocabulary appropriate for 10-year-olds"
- "Include no more than 15 questions"
- "The total should fit on one A4 page"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Better Approach |
|---|---|
| Too vague | Be specific about audience, format, and level |
| Too long | Focus on what matters; avoid unnecessary detail |
| No examples | Show what kind of output you want |
| No iteration | Refine your prompt based on the first result |

## Practice Exercise

Try converting this vague request into a well-structured prompt:

**Vague**: "Help me make a test"

**Better**: Think about what subject, grade level, test format, number of questions, difficulty level, and time limit you need, then include all of those in your prompt.

The more you practice, the more naturally effective prompting will come to you.

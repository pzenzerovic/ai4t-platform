## What Does "Human in the Loop" Mean?

In the context of AI in education, "human in the loop" means that teachers maintain oversight and final decision-making authority over AI-generated outputs and recommendations. AI assists; humans decide.

## Why This Principle Matters

### AI Can Be Wrong

As we learned in earlier lessons, AI can hallucinate — generating confident but incorrect information. A human reviewer catches these errors before they reach students.

### Context Requires Human Judgment

AI doesn't know your specific students, your school culture, or the nuances of your classroom. A worksheet that looks perfect on paper might be inappropriate for your particular group of students.

### Accountability

When AI-generated content is used in education, the teacher remains responsible for what students receive. This responsibility cannot be delegated to an algorithm.

## Practical Applications

### Reviewing AI-Generated Materials

Before using any AI-generated content:

1. **Read it thoroughly** — don't skim
2. **Check facts** — verify dates, statistics, and claims
3. **Assess appropriateness** — consider your students' age, background, and needs
4. **Edit freely** — treat AI output as a draft, not a finished product

### Maintaining Professional Judgment

AI might suggest that a student needs remedial work based on test scores, but you know that student was sick that day. AI recommendations should inform, not dictate, your decisions.

### Teaching Students About Oversight

Help students understand why human oversight matters:

- Share examples of AI errors you've caught
- Practice evaluating AI output together
- Discuss why critical thinking is more important than ever

## The Balance

The goal is not to distrust AI or avoid it. The goal is to use AI as a powerful tool while maintaining the professional judgment that makes you an effective teacher. Think of AI as a highly capable assistant who needs supervision — not a replacement for your expertise.
